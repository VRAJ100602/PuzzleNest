Sudoku
Fill a 9×9 grid so every row, column, and 3×3 box contains the digits 1–9 exactly once.
EasyMediumHardExpert
Play Now →

The Rules

  • Each row must contain 1–9 with no repeats.
  • Each column must contain 1–9 with no repeats.
  • Each of the nine 3×3 boxes must contain 1–9 with no repeats.
  • Grey cells are given clues — you cannot change them.

How to Play

Step 1 — Click any empty white cell to select it (it turns gold).

Step 2 — Press a number key 1–9 or click the number pad to fill it. Green = correct, red = wrong.

Step 3 — Use ✏️ Pencil mode to jot candidate numbers in small text without committing.

Step 4 — Navigate with arrow keys. Press ⌫ to erase.

The 3×3 box already has 5,3,6,7,2,1,9,8 — the only missing digit is 4. Type 4!

Expert Tips

Naked Singles: When only one number can go in a cell, fill it immediately.
Hidden Singles: If a digit can only go in one cell within a row/column/box, place it there.
Pencil marks: Use pencil mode to list candidates; eliminate them as you find singles.
Crossword
Fill letters into a grid guided by numbered across and down clues.
Medium
Play Now →

The Rules

  • Black cells are walls — you never type in them.
  • White cells form words that match the numbered clues.
  • Words read left-to-right (Across) or top-to-bottom (Down).

How to Play

Click a white cell to select it. The current word highlights in cream.

Click the same cell again (or press Tab) to toggle between Across and Down direction.

Type letters — the cursor advances automatically. ⌫ erases and retreats. Arrow keys move one cell.

Use the clue list below the grid to jump to any word.

Tips

Short words first: 3-letter words have fewer possibilities — fill them to unlock intersecting longer words.
Check intersections: Every cell belongs to both an Across and a Down word; a confirmed letter helps both.
Pipes
Rotate pipe segments to create a continuous flow from source ◉ to sink ◎.
EasyMediumHard
Play Now →

The Rules

  • Each cell contains a pipe piece: straight ─, corner ┗, T-junction ┳, or cross ╋.
  • Click any piece to rotate it 90° clockwise.
  • Connected cells turn green. You win when the entire network is connected and the source links to the sink.

How to Play

Start from the dark green source cell (◉) or the red sink cell (◎) and work outward. The connected path highlights green as you rotate.

Corner pieces only connect on two adjacent sides. T-junctions connect three sides. Use these shapes as constraints to deduce orientations logically.
Mosaic (Fill-a-Pix)
Fill cells black or leave them white so each clue number equals the count of filled cells in its 3×3 neighbourhood.
EasyMediumHard
Play Now →

The Rules

  • Every clue cell has a number 0–9.
  • That number = how many of the (up to 9) cells in the 3×3 block centred on it are filled black — including the clue cell itself.
  • A clue of 0 means all 9 neighbours are white. A clue of 9 means all 9 are black.

How to Play

Left-click a cell to fill it black. Right-click (or Shift+click) to mark it with a dot (known empty). Click again to clear.

Shikaku
Divide the grid into rectangles. Each rectangle contains exactly one number equal to its area.
MediumHard
Play Now →

The Rules

  • Every cell belongs to exactly one rectangle.
  • Each rectangle contains exactly one number.
  • That number equals the area (width × height) of the rectangle.
  • Rectangles must have straight sides (no L-shapes).

How to Play

Click and drag across cells to draw a rectangle. Release to commit. Right-click a cell to erase its rectangle.

Prime numbers (2, 3, 5, 7) can only form one rectangle shape (e.g. a "2" must be 1×2 or 2×1). Start with these!
Large numbers near corners constrain the rectangle heavily — it can only extend inward.
LITS
Shade exactly one tetromino per region. All shaded cells connect and no 2×2 block is fully shaded.
Hard
Play Now →

The Four Tetrominoes

  • L — Three in a line + one cell on the end, bent 90°
  • I — Four cells in a straight line
  • T — Three in a line + one cell sticking out from the middle
  • S — Two cells, then two more offset by one (zigzag)

The Rules

  • Every coloured region must contain exactly one shaded tetromino (4 shaded cells).
  • Two tetrominoes of the same letter type may not share an edge (they can touch diagonally).
  • All shaded cells across the whole grid must form one connected group.
  • No 2×2 block of cells may be entirely shaded.

How to Play

Click cells to shade (black) or unshade (colour). The shape label (L/I/T/S) shown in the top-right corner of each region is your target shape. Use ✓ Check to validate your solution.

Start with small regions — a region of exactly 4 cells must be entirely shaded, and the shape is forced.
Snap (Memory Match)
Flip cards two at a time to find all matching emoji pairs.
EasyMediumHard
Play Now →

The Rules

  • All cards start face-down (showing ?).
  • Click any card to flip it face-up.
  • Click a second card. If they match — they stay face-up (green). If they don't — both flip back after a short delay.
  • Find all pairs to win. Fewest moves = higher score.

Tips

Memory: Even when cards flip back, remember their positions! Next time you flip one, you'll know where its partner is.
Strategy: On your second flip, always pick a card you've already seen if you know its match — don't flip unknowns if you can score a pair.
Atoms (Chain Reaction)
2-player turn-based. Place atoms — when a cell overflows it explodes, converting neighbours to your colour.
Hard
Play Now →

Critical Mass

  • Corner cells — critical mass of 2
  • Edge cells — critical mass of 3
  • Centre cells — critical mass of 4

When a cell reaches its critical mass, it explodes: all its atoms disappear and each orthogonal neighbour gains one atom (and takes your colour). This can chain-react!

How to Play

Players alternate. Click any empty cell or your own cell to add one atom. You cannot click the opponent's cells.

The last player with atoms on the board wins.

Corners are powerful — a corner only needs 2 atoms to explode, so you can chain explosions cheaply. Build pressure near corners first.
Critical cells glow — cells near their critical mass are highlighted. Click them to trigger chain reactions on your turn.
Kings
Place the required number of kings on the chess board so none attacks another.
EasyMediumHard
Play Now →

The Rules

  • A king attacks all 8 adjacent cells (horizontally, vertically, and diagonally).
  • No two kings may be on squares that attack each other.
  • Some kings are pre-placed (green) — you cannot move them.
  • Some cells are blocked (black) — you cannot place there.
  • Place exactly the number shown at the top.

How to Play

Click any empty cell to place a king (♛). Click again to remove it. Cells that are attacked by existing kings turn red — avoid placing there.

Spacing rule: Two kings need at least one empty cell between them in every direction. Think of each king as occupying a 3×3 zone.
Mambo (Hidato)
Fill in missing numbers so that consecutive integers form a connected path (including diagonals).
EasyMediumHard
Play Now →

The Rules

  • Every integer from 1 to N appears exactly once.
  • Consecutive numbers (e.g. 5 and 6) must be in cells that touch — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • Black cells are blocked — never played.
  • Gold numbers are given clues — don't change them.

How to Play

Click an empty white cell to select it (gold border). Then use the number pad or type a number to fill it. Cells with a valid path to their neighbours glow green; wrong placements turn red.

Work between givens: Find two nearby given numbers like 7 and 11 — you need to fit 8, 9, 10 in cells that connect them. Count available cells and plan the route.
